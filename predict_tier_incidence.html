Predicting tier ratings with trafficking incidence

April 11, 2016

The seriousness of trafficking in a country influences its tier rating. Countries are more likely to receive worse tier ratings when they act as either an origin or a destination country. This likely shows that the TIP report picks up on increases in the sending and receiving of trafficked people. Conversely, the report does not appear to condemn countries that see increases in transitory traffickingâ€”these countries are more likely to receive better tier ratings as trafficking through their borders worsens.
